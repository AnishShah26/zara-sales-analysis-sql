# Project Summary — Zara Sales Analysis SQL

## Purpose

The project analyses Zara sales data using SQL to understand revenue drivers, product performance, price range demand, promotion impact, seasonality and store placement.

## Dataset

- Source: Kaggle Zara Sales Dataset
- Records: 252 rows
- Unique products: 194
- Key fields: product name, price, sales volume, promotion, seasonality, product position and section

## Main Business Questions

1. What is the total revenue and sales volume?
2. Which products sell the most?
3. Which products generate the highest revenue?
4. Which products underperform and may need discounts?
5. Do promotions improve sales and revenue?
6. Which price range performs best?
7. How do seasonality and product placement affect sales?

## Key Results

| Metric | Result |
|---|---:|
| Total revenue | $38,988,476.48 |
| Total sales volume | 459,573 |
| Unique products | 194 |
| Average price | $86.25 |
| Highest-priced product | CROPPED LEATHER JACKET |
| Lowest-priced product | HIGH COLLAR KNIT SWEATER |

## Business Interpretation

The strongest commercial opportunity is the mid-price product range, especially the 50–100 band, which combines high demand with strong revenue contribution. Promotions are useful but should be evaluated against margin and revenue, not only units sold. Low-selling products should be reviewed for discounts, improved placement, better campaign support or range rationalisation.
